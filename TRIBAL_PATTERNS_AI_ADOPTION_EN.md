## Synthesis with Piaget: Cognitive + Tribal Transformation

### The Critical Difference: Assimilation vs Accommodation

**Assimilation** (Piaget):
- You add new information to existing cognitive structures
- Example: "I learn a new tool in my current workflow"
- AI = feature added to old work

**Accommodation** (Piaget):
- You transform fundamental cognitive structures to integrate the new
- Example: "I think differently about what expertise means"
- AI = completely different way of conceptualizing work

### Why AI Adoption Fails

**The Problem:**
Organizations treat transformation as **assimilation** ("learn the new tool"), when reality requires **accommodation** ("think differently about what expertise means").

**The Result:**
Classic training fails because:
1. It addresses assimilation (feature demo, tutorial, documentation)
2. It ignores the need for accommodation (fundamental cognitive restructuring)
3. It doesn't recognize tribal structure destruction (identity collapse)

### Double Disruption

**Combining Piaget + L√©vi-Strauss:**

AI adoption simultaneously disrupts:
1. **Thinking** (cognitive restructuring - Piaget)
2. **Identity** (tribal structure collapse - L√©vi-Strauss)

**That's why resistance is so visceral** - it's not simple "fear of change," but **double existential threat**:
- "I no longer know how to think about my work" (cognitive disorientation)
- "I no longer know who I am in the organization" (tribal displacement)

**Adaptation phases:**
